Cold Reading: the Barnum Effect

The Truth About “Mind Reading”

Barnum Statements are also known as Assimilation Bias or the Self Reference Phenomenon

  1. How it began

    1940's. Behavioral psychologist Bertram R Forer got 39 students together and gave them a personality test. Some weeks later he provided them each with a profile. He didn’t tell them they’d all been given the same personality profile. They thought the profiles were individualized based on the personality test. In fact, Forer had copied most of the profile’s content from an astrology book! He then asked them to check how accurate they thought their ‘individual’ profiles were. They gave a score of 4.3 out of 5 on average, which equates to a whopping 85.2% satisfaction rate. The test subjects were ready and willing to accept generalized descriptions of their personality as long as they would fit into their world view and belief system.
    Conclusion: People will believe that certain vague statements fit their personality if they are vague enough.

  2. The Original Barnum Statements

    1. You have a great need for other people to like and admire you.
    2. You have a tendency to be critical of yourself.
    3. You have a great deal of unused capacity which you have not turned to your advantage.
    4. While you have some personality weaknesses, you are generally able to compensate for them.
    5. Your sexual adjustment has presented problems for you.
    6. Disciplined and self-controlled outside, you tend to be worrisome and insecure inside.
    7. At times you have serious doubts as to whether you have made the right decision or done the right thing.
    8. You prefer a certain amount of change and variety and become dissatisfied when hemmed in by restrictions and limitations.
    9. You pride yourself as an independent thinker and do not accept others' statements without satisfactory proof.
    10. You have found it unwise to be too frank in revealing yourself to others.
    11. At times you are extroverted, affable, sociable, while at other times you are introverted, wary, reserved.
    12. Some of your aspirations tend to be pretty unrealistic.
    13. Security is one of your major goals in life.

  3. The Barnum Effect

    These are “truisms” that apply to almost everyone. You fit these statements to yourself. However, Barnum Statements always involve very “personal” aspects of the target. This way, they feel personalized to the person that you want to deliver to.

  4. Some Rules to follow

    • avoid Absolutes

      • Instead, use words that can be interpreted in different ways, such as: sometimes, somewhat, now and then, often, at times.

    • sound 100% convincing

      • To sound convincing, you’ve got to BE convincing. If you believe in what you’re saying you’ll give off a confident, trustworthy vibe. When someone disagrees with a Barnum Statement: Move on to the next Barnum Statement quickly. Don’t explain yourself. Just keep plowing!

    • the “Build-Up” is important
